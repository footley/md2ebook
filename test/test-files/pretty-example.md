This is Chapter 1
-----------------

This chapter simply contains examples of the standard things you might like 
to do in a book; bold, italics, urls etc. Not to mention a few things you may 
never find a use for, in any case they're there lurking, waiting for you to 
rest for just one moment.

Now we have another paragraph, this one with more text and interspesed with 
**bold** undertakings which shouldn't be _ignored_. Really this is fun times, 
everyone should have a go, its just about the _best_ time you can have with 
your pants on!

This is [an example](http://images.google.com/images?num=30&q=larry+bird "popup rollover") inline link.

Please note that each paragraph is seperated by an entire line. 
This is a requirement and should not be forgotten. The reason for this is that 
you can place single new lines in your txt doc to make it easier to read, 
if you like, and they will still flow correctly.

> This is a block quote, its a handy way to make a piece of text more 
> prominent in your story. I love them dearly. Lets add some more text to 
> this to make it a little longer. **Note that these don't work on the kindle 
> (they just appear as normal paragraphs).**

> > Then we might have a nested block quote! Wow, this bad boy is steaming hot
> > its almost as if we were playing with fire!!

> Thats quite enough exclamation points, we should probably calm down a little 
> here, lets drop back to level one.

Hey! Lets have an ordered list! (the actual numbers used here don't matter) 
you can have all 1. 1. if you like the output will make sense of it.

8. item onnnnnnne
1. item the second!
5. ...
3. and profit.

How about bullet points?

* pew pew

as the mobi, epub and pdf output will have page breaks but whatever, its 
entierly up to you whether its used or not!

would use these, never the less they are available!
